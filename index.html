Why ERA Exists

Responding to South Sudan's most pressing challenges

South Sudan continues to face widespread poverty, conflict, displacement, food insecurity, climate shocks, and limited access to education and healthcare. Women, youth, and persons with disabilities are disproportionately affected.

ERA-SS exists to empower communities by creating equitable access to opportunities, resources, and essential services. We work alongside communities to build resilience, promote peace, and foster sustainable development.

Key challenges we address

  • Poverty & inequality
  • Conflict & displacement
  • Food insecurity & malnutrition
  • Limited education & health services
  • Youth unemployment
  • Gender-based violence
  • Climate change impacts
About ERA-SS

A national movement for equity and dignity

Equity Resource Aid – South Sudan (ERA-SS) is a non-profit national NGO registered with the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission (RRC) on 10 June 2026. We are human-centred, evidence-based, and committed to sustainable development aligned with the SDGs and South Sudan Vision 2030.

Framework

9 Foundational Aims & 10 Strategic Objectives

Aims

  • Empower, educate and inspire children and all citizens to be responsible patriots.
  • Ensure citizens occupy the centre stage in the development of South Sudan.
  • Prepare mindsets in line with national cohesion.
  • Unify all regions and enhance national integration.
  • Serve as an entity for inclusion and tolerance in nation‑building.
  • Complement the government's efforts in making South Sudan better for all citizens.
  • Ensure multi‑dimensional mobilisations for the nation's building and sustenance.
  • Promote the relevance of non‑violence to children and young people.
  • Raise awareness about environmental safety, GBV, and accessibility to health education.

Objectives

  • Develop mutual relations and brotherhood through peacebuilding and reconciliation.
  • Launch awareness programs in peace, education, health, WASH, civic education, human rights, gender, and children.
  • Promote creative youth activities and sports for peace and security.
  • Carry out Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH) programs for those in need.
  • Cooperate in managing water schemes in working areas.
  • Promote community security and community policing programs.
  • Enhance indigenous well‑being via poverty alleviation programs.
  • Repair and maintain feeder roads with local structures.
  • Launch programs for women, children, the elderly, the helpless, and persons with disabilities.
  • Implement environmental conservation, food security, and economic stability projects.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is ERA-SS?

ERA-SS is a registered national non‑profit NGO working across South Sudan to empower communities through education, peacebuilding, livelihoods, and inclusive development.

Where does ERA work?

We work nationwide, partnering with local communities, government institutions, and civil society to reach the most vulnerable.

How can I partner with ERA?

You can reach us via email at equityresourceaid.net@gmail.com or through our contact form. We welcome collaboration with donors, agencies, and community organizations.

How can I volunteer?

Send us a message expressing your interest and skills. We’ll connect you with opportunities to contribute.

How can I support your work?

You can donate, partner, or volunteer. Every contribution helps us expand our reach and deepen our impact.

How do you ensure transparency?

We adhere to strict accountability standards, maintain financial records, and will publish annual reports as we grow. Documents are available upon request.
